Q1: What does a hard reset do?

A1: A hard reset is a process used to erase all data and settings from a device, restoring it back to its original factory settings. It is sometimes referred to as a master reset. On an Android device, a hard reset can help fix software issues and other problems like frozen screens or slow performance.

Q2: What is the difference between a hard reset and a factory reset?

A2: The difference between a hard reset and a factory reset is that a hard reset will reset the device to its original settings, while a factory reset will restore the device back to its original state as it was when it left the factory. While a hard reset can help with software issues, a factory reset is more suitable for hardware issues.

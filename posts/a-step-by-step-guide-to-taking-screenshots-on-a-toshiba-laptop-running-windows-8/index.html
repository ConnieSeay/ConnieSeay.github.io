Let’s start by talking about how to take screenshots with just your keyboard. To do this, locate the “PrtSc” key on your keyboard - it should be to the right of the “F12” key. Taking screenshots with the keyboard is great, but if you want to be able to capture specific areas of your screen, then you’ll need to use the Windows “Snipping Tool.” To open the Snipping Tool, press the “Windows Key + S” and type “Snipping Tool” into the search bar. Once the Snipping Tool is open, you can select any rectangular area of your screen and press the “Save” button - the image will be saved to your laptop’s “Pictures” folder as a PNG image file, so you can then view or share it.

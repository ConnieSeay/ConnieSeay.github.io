2. Capturing Screenshots with Home & Volume Buttons#

Taking screenshots on the iPad Pro 11 is a great way to capture what’s on your display. Knowing how to take screenshots on an iPad Pro 11 can be useful in certain situations, such as troubleshooting, showing off something you found on the internet, or capturing a funny moment. There are three ways to take screenshots on an iPad Pro 11. You can press the Power/Sleep button and the Volume Up button simultaneously, use the AssistiveTouch menu, or use the Apple Pencil.

Pressing the Power/Sleep button and the Volume Up button simultaneously is the most common way to take a screenshot on an iPad Pro 11. Simply press and hold these two buttons until the display flashes white and you hear a shutter sound. If you don’t hear the shutter sound, click the mute button to turn it on. The screenshot will be saved to your Photos app.

You can also use the AssistiveTouch menu to take a screenshot on your iPad Pro 11. Go to Settings, then Accessibility, then AssistiveTouch. Turn on the AssistiveTouch menu, and you’ll see a small circle appear on your display. Tap the circle, then tap Device, then More, then Screenshot. This will take a screenshot and save it to your Photos app.

Finally, you can use the Apple Pencil to take a screenshot on your iPad Pro 11. Tap and hold the Apple Pencil against the display until you see a menu appear. Tap the screenshot option and your screenshot will be saved to your Photos app.

These are all the different ways to take a screenshot on an iPad Pro 11. Depending on what you’re trying to accomplish, one of these methods may be more suitable than the others.

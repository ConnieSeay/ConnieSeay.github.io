I. How to Check RAM on Android Tablet#

You may not be sure how much RAM your Android tablet has or if it’s enough for the applications you’d like to use. Knowing how to check the RAM on your Android tablet can be helpful in diagnosing unwanted issues or in deciding if you need to upgrade the device. Here are 6 simple steps to check your tablet’s RAM.

First, access your device settings. From there, select the “Device Information” menu. This will provide basic information about your tablet. Most will show the total amount of RAM as well as the amount of RAM currently available.

Second, open the app manager. This will give you a full list of applications installed on your device. With many tablets, you can select any app in the list and it will provide detailed information on that specific application. Specifically, it will show you the amount of RAM being used by each app currently running in the background.

Third, use a real-time monitoring app. There are numerous applications available for Android to monitor RAM usage in real-time. You can then observe the fluctuations of RAM your device is experiencing over time.

Fourth, use a benchmarking app. A benchmarking app allows users to measure and compare the performance of their devices in relation to other devices. This is helpful in seeing how much RAM your device is using relative to other devices with the same configuration.

Fifth, use the task manager. Many manufacturers include a task manager on their Android devices, which will show the amount of RAM being used by open applications in the background.

Finally, compare the RAM specs of your device with the specs of other devices. This will provide a good indication of the amount of RAM your tablet has. It can also provide insight on how much RAM certain apps require on your device.
